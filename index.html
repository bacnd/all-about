Whether it’s the skyscrapers and bright lights of the big city, the jaw-dropping natural wonders of the remote countryside, mouth-watering cuisine found in local areas, or anything in between, there’s an endless number of reasons to wander Japan. You never know what you might discover along the way!

Things to Do

Plan Your Trip

  • Moving Art in Greater Tokyo

    Japan has a rich history of traditional, modern, and experiential art, and Greater Tokyo is one of the best places to dive in! On this two-day course, visitors will explore traditional and modern marvels in Tokyo, before heading to Kanagawa Prefecture to explore the country’s natural beauty.

  • Epic Outdoor Adventures

    After experiencing all the big city has to offer, it’s great to stretch your legs and explore some of the incredible outdoor locations accessible from Tokyo! Both Chiba and Ibaraki are home to stunning landscapes that offer visitors a variety of unique outdoor activities.

  • Explore a Wild Wonderland

    Wander outside of Tokyo to a wilder part of the country, where locals have long lived in harmony with nature. The unspoiled landscapes of Gunma and Niigata are home to an incredible variety of exciting outdoor activities, living art that changes with the seasons, and local delicacies that are sure to please.

  • A Spiritual Retreat to Japan

    Central Japan is home to some truly breathtaking, unspoiled landscapes just begging to be explored. As such, it is a mecca for those looking to enjoy the great outdoors, especially Yamanashi Prefecture’s wine country, and the majestic peaks found in Nagano. It is also home to many sacred sites that can help visitors get in touch with Japanese spiritual traditions.

  • Discover Samurai Spirit

    Journey north of Tokyo to Fukushima Prefecture, the gateway to Japan’s northeastern region of Tohoku. There you’ll find the samurai spirit alive and well, and have the opportunity to explore one of the most enduring aspects of Japanese history. You’ll also find unspoiled nature home to a wealth of thrilling outdoor activities.

  • Beauty Just North of Tokyo

    You don’t have to go very far from the big city to take a deep dive into unique aspects of Japanese history, culture, and natural beauty. Saitama and Tochigi prefectures are just north of Tokyo, and offer travelers the chance to explore gorgeous locales, stroll retro city streets, and get in touch with enduring Japanese traditions.

Transportation

If you truly want to wander in and around Tokyo, there’s no better way to get around than with JR EAST. With an incredible rail network that extends from Tokyo to Hokkaido, and all the prefectures in between, there are a variety of useful and affordable passes to suit your travel needs. Whether you’re zooming from prefecture to prefecture on Shinkansen bullet trains, or slowly roaming on quaint local lines, JR EAST makes it easy to make the most of your time and money while you explore the Land of the Rising Sun.
