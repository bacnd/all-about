Head to Japan’s former capital city of Kamakura to get your very own piece of history: a handcrafted name stamp that will let you recall your travels across Japan with every use.

Make your mark on Kamakura with a personalized name seal thanks to the help of Kamakura Mind!

In Kamakura, Japan’s ancient capital from 1185 to 1333, it’s easy to get caught up in the lively hustle and bustle of its most popular sightseeing spots like the giant Buddha, the beautiful beachside, or the grand Tsurugaoka Hachiman Shrine. But if you want to dive a little deeper into Kamakura’s rich culture, Kamakura Mind offers many experiences that give a nuanced glimpse into the traditions of the city. And if you want a piece of culture that you can take home with you, try your hand at making your very own hanko, or personal seal!

Hanko are used in various aspects of Japanese life and are basically the equivalent of a signature. The Chinese characters used in Japanese names can be quite intricate, so these small seals pack a surprisingly large amount of detail. Visitors can choose to have their names written in Chinese characters, or in katakana or hiragana, the other two writing systems for Japan’s syllabic alphabet.

Once you’ve settled on which you would like, you get to start designing your seal. After that, it’s just a matter of having the details carved out by Kamakura Mind’s skilled artisans, Kamakura Hanko. The best part of the experience is that you’ll have your very own hanko ready to take home on the same day! And this is just of the many unique experiences Kamakura Mind has on offer.
