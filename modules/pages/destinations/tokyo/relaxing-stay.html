The best travel plans all center around finding appropriate accommodation. Whether you’re looking for a traditional experience or a more modern place to crash, Tokyo has no shortage of options for every lifestyle.

Find the best place to lay your head while visiting Tokyo, regardless of your travel style.

Tokyo has a certain allure as a city. The typical visitor can get lost in the hustle and bustle of trains and taxis zooming: the usual, overwhelming metropolis scene. Yet a couple of paces down an unmarked side street might make you feel like you’ve teleported to a different town, or another era even. And when you’re ready to lay your head down, Tokyo has no shortage of great accommodation options—in fact, it has well over 1,000, and has the most out of any city in Japan—meaning it’s easy to get a restful night’s sleep no matter your sleep style.

To experience luxury alongside Japanese tradition, consider staying at a ryokan, or Japanese inn. These run the gamut from facilities that have been practicing their hospitality for decades or more, to more modern establishments that take the fundamentals and bring them to the present-day. Regardless, you can expect immaculate service, delicious food, and (often) a soak in a bath!

There are also a variety of creative, contemporary hotels to suit your travel needs. Whether you’re looking for a modern and comfortable take on Japan’s unique capsule hotels, or want to stay in a bunk where you can cozy up with a good book, or want to wine and dine at Michelin-starred in-house restaurants, the choice is yours! And that is just the tip of the iceberg! With accommodation options to suit any taste or travel style, choosing where to stay can be as fun as exploring the city itself.

City Stay at Tokyo Hotels

One of the best ways to ensure a great trip is to stay at a great hotel, which Tokyo has in abundance! Whether you’re looking for a comfy, budget hotel to make your base or want to enjoy a luxurious staycation, there are options to suit any taste and budget.
