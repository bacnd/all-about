Nagano

More than just a world-class haven for winter sports, Nagano is a prefecture that offers a unique blend of cultural and historic experiences. Whether strolling the streets of Japan’s ancient highways, basking in the majesty of its many towering mountains, or admiring its crow-black castle and numerous temples, there’s a wealth of opportunity for any aspiring wanderer.

History

Strolling the Atmospheric Streets of Narai-Juku

Walk along a route once used by pilgrims, aristocrats, and merchants in ancient Japan as you wander the Nagano streets of Narai-juku, one of the best-preserved stations of the Nakasendo route that connected Tokyo to Kyoto.

Art & Culture

Behold the Crow-Black Beauty of Matsumoto Castle

Only two hours from Tokyo by train lies Matsumoto, home to one of the best-preserved castles in Japan. And what makes this fact even more stunning is the castle is resplendent in black, a rare color for a Japanese castle. It’s one you have to see with your own eyes.

Hit the Fabled Powder Slopes of Hakuba

As any snow junkie in Japan might tell you, few locales on earth can compare to the powder slopes of the Land of the Rising Sun. And the Happo-One resort is up there with the very best.

Experience Buddhist Cuisine and Traditions at Zenkoji Temple

Live the life of a Buddhist monk, from early morning meditation to sampling the delectable vegetarian cuisine, while experiencing the peaceful surroundings of Zenkoji Temple in Nagano.
