Go wild at Nasu Animal Kingdom, the perfect place to make new animal friends while learning more about the rare and wonderful creatures that call this area of Tochigi home.

Unleash your inner beast at Tochigi’s Animal Kingdom!

Situated in Tochigi’s scenic hot-spring resort area, the Nasu Animal Kingdom is a diverse animal park home to a huge variety of critters and creatures from all over. More of a large, animal-filled farm than a zoo, Nasu Animal Kingdom offers a new way to interact with some of the world’s most fascinating animals in a welcoming, family-friendly environment. Here you can meet the park’s popular, and very social, capybaras before attending one of the regular animal shows.

The 106-acre park is split into two zones, the Farm Zone and Town Zone. The Farm Zone houses the animals while Town Zone is home to restaurants, shopping facilities and even an onsen. One place of interest is the captivating Capybara Forest, an enclosure featuring multiple baths (for the capybaras only) and plenty of space to wander around while feeding your new fuzzy friends.

Nearby you’ll find Alpaca Hill and the curly-haired alpacas just waiting to be fed and petted. There’s also an on-site tropical rainforest where guests are welcome to admire sleepy sloths and cheeky monkeys as well as families of colorful tropical birds fluttering over the enclosures housing horses, camels, and kangaroos. Educational and exciting animal shows run daily, including bird shows featuring eagles, hawks, horned owls, and other birds of prey. There also an adorable cat show and an aquatic show complete with air-flipping seals. However, the most extraordinary event has to be the New Zealand farm show, where you can marvel at the discipline and intelligence of the farm’s resident sheepdogs who guide sheep with grace and unforgettable class. (Please note that the schedule of shows is subject to change in 2021)

Nasu Animal Kingdom

With a wealth of diverse animals and performances to enjoy, Nasu Animal Kingdom offers wildlife fun for the whole family. With free shuttle service from Nasushiobara Station, there’s no reason not to visit!
